Digitalfire Reference Library
A public ad-free, no-tracking, technical reference website for potters, educators, technicians and hobbyists in pottery and ceramic production. Since 2000. It is an SEO and AEO success story.

Details
The
Digitalfire Reference Library
is me,
Tony Hansen
. It is a public website at http://digitalfire.com/home, it exposes parts of the internal database. It has no ads, no analytics and no bots that follow you in or out. It is an online library of practical technical information about traditional
ceramics
for hobbyists, potters, education and manufacturing. It contains thousands of evolving pages about how to adjust and fix issues with materials, recipes and procedures. It is about understanding things from the physical and process levels down to the material, mineral and
chemistry
levels. It has been under constant construction since the mid-90s, and its pages have been Google top-tens since then. It is mobile-first, hierarchically organized, interlinked and searchable.
The
Digitalfire
online library is authored internally by my own
content management system
(
CMS
), pages for public consumption are assembled, by code, from that database. My CMS is now a combination of systems. I created the first part of it using DOS
Foresight
(which he wrote in the mid-1980s); it was a multi-user laboratory notebook product and enabled continuous compilation of lab testing data but also for authoring of technical content on many levels (e.g. chemistry,
mineralogy
, material, process, recipe, firing, etc). By the early 90s, custom code was publishing shared parts of it as a
PDF
file (The Magic of Fire book). The system evolved into the online
4Sight
CMS, which I still use; it had automatic publishing tools that created ~5000 pages of the library online until 2020. Starting in the late 1990s, I also began publishing plansmanclays.com (on my own) using similar techniques; it has developed into an information resource in parallel. I also incorporated authoring, sharing and publishing tools into
Insight-live
.com; my account there is now part of my CMS. Today, new publishing code creates a Bootstrap-based UI for a mobile-first experience with many new features. Throughout the time of development, I have published end-user versions of my software products (
desktop Insight
, Foresight, Insight-live.com), giving everyone many of the calculation and data management tools I use.
Page development at the Digitalfire library was primarily driven by my own body, glaze,
engobe
and material product development and the tech support I rendered in response to thousands of requests (from customers of our Digitalfire products and
Plainsman
clays). Instead of just answering, I research and often do my own testing and then weave the results into my internal CMS (either by adding information or modifying existing content). I then direct the person to the pages online (which are automatically produced or updated from the content I modified). These drivers have been the primary growth catalyst for decades - and the source of a lot of the photos (I always make sure I have a good camera or phone camera). An important benefit of this information source is that it is most often from potters. They face every production challenge. This is a very different situation than when a worker at a manufacturing plant has an issue - he/she generally has minimal knowledge and control of the overall process and is restricted in the available information and what can be shared. As a result, I often do not have the resources needed to do any applicable testing. That being said, customers often tell me their manufacturing facility could not exist if it were not for the Digitalfire library!

Magic of Fire book
This picture has its own page with more detail,
click here
to see it.
A book published by
Tony Hansen
(no longer available). It explained why we need to think about materials (and the bodies and glazes made from them) as more than just powders. They have physical, chemical and mineralogical presences, a knowledge of which provides better control in ceramic production. This was the first widely read book to show how, using this knowledge and
glaze chemistry
, readers could solve all sorts of problems. It showcased the real value of the oxide viewpoint in
ceramics
and explained how to use
Digitalfire Insight
and
Foresight
software in each scenario. From 2000-2014, the book was used as courseware in universities around the world. In 2015 it was superseded by the online
Digitalfire Reference Library
(which is a superset of its content).
