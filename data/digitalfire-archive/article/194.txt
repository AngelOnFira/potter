There are two ways: Weigh up a batch of the Cornwall substitute, mix it thoroughly, then treat it as a raw material (if you are confident enough in being able to shake the mix enough in a plastic bag to believe it is mixed well). Otherwise, retotal the Cornwall substitute to 20.3 and combine it with the above recipe. Actually, both of these approaches have a small issue. The
L3617
recipe contains materials have that an
LOI
, its total calculated
LOI
is 2.4%. That means the most correct amount to substitute for 20.3 is 20.3/(100-0.27/100) or 20.8.
