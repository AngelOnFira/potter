Fluorine Gas
Various material used in
ceramics
can generate fluorine gas during firing. This gas in hazardous, for example ILO says: Avoid all contact and in all cases contact a doctor.
For example, fluorspar contains alot of fluorine has a
TLV
(threshold limit value) of 2.5 milligrams per cubic meter of air breathed. By comparison
iron oxide
is considered a safe-to-use material at 5.0,
kaolin
is 2.0,
barium carbonate
is 0.5, quartz is 0.1-0.05.
Cryolite also contains a very large amount of fluorine and thus poses a specific hazard. Even
Cornwall Stone
, a widely used glaze material, can contain up to 2%.
Good kiln ventilation is essential. Over a period of time, fluorine gas will even etch windows in the kiln area until they are opaque like frosted glass. This is ample evidence of its presence.
